If you write for a living or as a hobby, there are a few habits you should consider adding to your process. There are plenty of suggestions you’ve probably heard of before, but as technology advances, it’s becoming more necessary than ever to form new regular patterns.

Many authors struggle with crucial elements that can halt their progress. The most common wall to climb is starting an article. Other issues include finding the right subjects to cover, building a consistent work style, and creating a format that properly engages an audience.

With the ideas in this article, you might find astounding progress in a short time period. Read on and see if any of them work for you.


Starting an Article and Forming Ideas

Staring at a blank page with a flashing cursor isn’t overly productive. In fact, this writing approach leaves authors vulnerable to distractions like phone notifications and unfinished chores in the back of their minds.

You can fight a rough start with a few easy to implement habits. If you already have your subject matter in mind, write it on a piece of paper or type it on an application that allows idea mapping. Then consider items or topics related to your concept.

When ideas pop into your mind during this brainstorming process, make sure to add them as bullet points under each bubble. If you think of a *car as an extended concept for your central theme, for example, list the parts underneath the title. This way, when you start writing, you can refer to the sheet for references and improve your flow.

*Here’s the example:

Car

  • Engine
  • Tires
  • Tailpipe
  • Headlights
  • Trunk
  • Windows


Getting Some Help

If these techniques for starting an article or creating ideas don’t work, it’s not shameful to seek assistance. Believe it or not, you can ask for help from an internet marketing agency. They often have expert copywriters and content creators that can work with you to find and organize concepts and themes and expand on your ideas.


Pomodoro

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management system that proposes 25 minute work chunks with 5 to 10 minute breaks in between. While it’s not a strategy exclusive for writing, it still works wonders for authors. 

You can download an application or program on your phone or computer to automate the process. Otherwise, consider setting an alarm for each period.


Similar Alternatives

If the Pomodoro technique doesn’t work for you, similar strategies might provide improvements for your writing. 

The Action Method focuses more on creating a list of tasks with in-depth explanations with unique time-frames. Gamification, another example, tries to make your work more enjoyable with goals, achievements, and visual changes as you progress.


Fighting Writer’s Block

Fighting writer’s block is essential for content creators that have high output requirements and strict deadlines. Certain habits can prevent those awkward moments where you stare blankly at the screen or other objects in the room.

Luckily, avoiding idling is easier than you might think. Try to implement an automatic response when you waste time. If you can't come up with ideas for five minutes, for example, go for a short walk, do a few push-ups, drink some water, or listen to a few songs. You should find it easier to create content if you stay consistent with this strategy.
