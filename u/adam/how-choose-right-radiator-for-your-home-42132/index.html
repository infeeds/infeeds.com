Buying a radiator used to be a fairly simple process: you just picked one that would sufficiently heat the room it was intended for. However, this is no longer the case. 

Due to many advancements in the radiator manufacturing and design industry, there are now a whole host of different designs that you can choose from for your home. 

This could, and should, be considered a positive step forward in the world of radiators; however, it does make the entire process of picking one for your home a little bit more challenging. 

That being said, if you know what you want in a radiator, then you should be able make a decision that is right for you and your home. 

So, without further delay, let’s take a look at how you can choose the right radiator for your property and start enjoying the benefits of a heated and stylish home, sooner rather than later. 

What Do You Need From Your Radiator? 

This may seem like a silly question. Obviously you want your new radiator to sufficiently heat your home. However, is this all that you want from your radiator? Or, do you also want it to fit in with the rest of your interior design style and act as another key feature in your home? 

It is crucial that you identify what it is that you want from your new radiator before you even start to look at purchasing one. 

Fortunately, you can now find radiators that are both practical and stylish, with warmrooms.co.uk stocking the ever-popular Zehnder radiators that are known for their functionality and innovative and modern look. 

Where are You Planning on Installing Your Radiator? 

It can be a good idea to spend some time in your home looking at where you could possibly hang your new radiator as this can be crucial in finding out what type of radiator you can purchase. 

You will also need to look at the type of wall you are planning to hang your radiator on as you will need to ensure that it can handle the weight of it. 

As a rule, it is advisable to not hang radiators behind large pieces of furniture as this will prevent the room from becoming adequately heated. 

Do You Need a Plumbed or Electric Radiator? 

If you are simply replacing an old radiator with a new one, then you will probably want to stick with the central heating system that you already have in your home. However, if you have just built a new space such as a loft conversion, extension or conservatory, you may wish to purchase an electric radiator as these are easier to install. 

Plumbed radiators rely on a boiler to heat hot water which is then circulated around your home via pipes, whereas electric radiators do not have a central control system. Instead, they are controlled in each individual room.

Each has its own advantages and disadvantages and it is entirely up to personal preference in terms of which one you choose for your home. 

What Type of Radiator Would You Prefer?

There are many different types of radiators on offer, meaning that you will be able to find the perfect one for your home and individual requirements. 

Some of the most popular styles of radiators include:

  • Central heating radiators 
  • Column radiators 
  • Designer radiators
  • Vertical radiators
  • Towel radiators 
  • Electric radiators 
  • Cast iron radiators 
  • Flat panel radiators 
  • Space saving radiators 

You also have the option to invest in radiator covers or cabinets which allow you to stylishly disguise your radiators, as well as act as an attractive feature in a room.
