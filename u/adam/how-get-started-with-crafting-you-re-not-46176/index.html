Crafts can be a great way to spend your time and if you put in enough practice, you could get really good at making something. But if you’re not naturally artistic, you might be worried about getting into crafts. Thankfully, crafting is for everybody so don’t worry if you’re not Picasso - chances are you’ll find something that you enjoy doing. And, don’t forget that art and craft really is subjective so as long as you like it, that’s the only thing that matters. If you’re trying to tap into the artistic and creative side of yourself, here are some top tips to get you started. 

Get Some Discounted Supplies:

Art and craft supplies can be expensive and for many people who are new to crafting and not sure where to start or what kind of crafts they’re going to enjoy the most, this can be off-putting. After all, you don’t want to spend a hundred dollars on a load of paints and canvases only to find that you really don’t enjoy painting as much as you thought you would. These JOANN coupons can really come in handy if you want to try out some different crafts; you can use JOANN coupons to get discounts on a range of materials and supplies from knitting to card making, so figuring out what you enjoy doesn’t have to break the bank. 

Watch Some Tutorials:

Everything has the potential to seem difficult if you’ve never done it before so don’t be too worried if you want to take up knitting but haven’t got the faintest clue how to cast the wool onto the needle. Remember that even the most talented of crafters who make the most intricate and detailed projects look like a piece of cake have had to start somewhere. You might find it useful to watch some YouTube tutorials designed for beginners; there are plenty of options to help you get to grips with the basics of any craft that you decide to try your hand at.

Pick Something That You Enjoy:

Don’t think that you have to take up a certain craft just because it’s the latest ‘in’ thing or because your friends and family are doing it. Crafting is a hobby, and hobbies should be fun and enjoyable so go for something that you like. If you’re struggling for ideas, think about what you used to do as a child and go from there. 

Practice!

Whether you decide to make handmade greetings cards, paint acrylic canvases, make your own clothes or knit outfits for your dog, remember that practice makes perfect. The first few times that you try probably won’t be worth writing home about but you should still be proud of yourself because already, you’re better at it than you were before you started! The more you practice your craft, the better you’ll become at it, so learn new things and more importantly, have fun. 

If you’re not a naturally artistic person, taking up crafting might feel daunting. But if you’ve always wanted to have a go at making things, the good news is that anybody can become a fantastic crafter with a bit of practice.
