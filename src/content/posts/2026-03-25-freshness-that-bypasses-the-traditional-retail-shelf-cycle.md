There is definitely something really delightful about opening the fridge on a midweek evening and finding kale or blueberries that still look stunningly fresh as on the first day of their arrival. It transforms the idea of preparing food from a dull task to a fun artistic experiment. Having such a regular supply of fresh produce in your kitchen mainly relies on the speed with which those fruits and vegetables travel from the farm to your doorstep.

Food will not only taste better but also stay fresh longer if the trip from the farm is made shorter. No more lineups at supermarkets, a novel and more dependable way to organise the weekly grocery will be uncovered. It is a small change that emphasises fine living, guaranteeing that each component will be the best possible one when it is brought to the dining table.

## **Freshness That Bypasses The Traditional Retail Shelf Cycle**  
The tale of fruits and vegetables travelling from farm to kitchen table is an interesting one. In many supermarket formats, produce is constantly in motion - being shipped, being stored in the backroom and being placed on the rack. The freshness and nutrition of these items are already on the decline when they are brought home. Figuring out how to cut down on that time is the secret to having food that stays fresh and tasty all week.  

Today's logistics is such that you can do away with those long intermediaries altogether. The move to a more direct approach to food sourcing means that perishables now spend less time in a warehouse and more time in your refrigerator. In addition to enhancing the flavour of meals, the change can also revolutionise the way a household works with its weekly supplies.
