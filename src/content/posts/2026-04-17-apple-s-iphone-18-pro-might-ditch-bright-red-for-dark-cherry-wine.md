Apple's color choices for the iPhone have always felt like a cultural statement. Last year it was cosmic orange. This year, if rumors are to be believed, the company is ready to trade brightness for sophistication.

According to reporting from Macworld, which cites a source within Apple's supply chain, the upcoming iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max will debut in a dark cherry shade that leans more wine than red. The color allegedly corresponds to Pantone 6076, and it represents a meaningful shift from the vibrant hues Apple has been leaning on recently.

This aligns with earlier reporting from Bloomberg's Mark Gurman, who suggested Apple was considering red as its next bold color direction. But dark cherry feels like the more refined execution of that idea. It's the kind of color that photographs differently depending on the light, which probably appeals to Apple's design sensibilities.

## The Supply Chain Rumor Mill Keeps Spinning

Here's where we need to pump the brakes a little. None of this is official. Apple hasn't confirmed the iPhone 18 Pro, the Pro Max, or anything else on this timeline. The source behind Macworld's reporting is unnamed and connected to the supply chain, which means it's credible but unverified.

That said, Macworld's source did correctly predict the cosmic orange color for the iPhone 17 Pro last year, which gives this latest leak some weight. The source also claims Apple is experimenting with light blue (Pantone 2121), dark gray (Pantone 426C), and a silver variation of the current iPhone 17 Pro.

Noticeably absent from this palette is black, which contradicts an earlier rumor suggesting Apple would bring back a solid black option. Whether that's intentional design philosophy or just uncertainty in the rumor cycle remains unclear.

## A Foldable Phone Enters the Chat

The same source told Macworld that Apple's long-awaited foldable phone will also launch in September, alongside the new Pro models. The foldable's color options are reportedly more limited, with Apple considering a classic silver-and-white finish and an indigo shade similar to the deep blue found on the iPhone 17 Pro.

Apple still hasn't publicly named this foldable. Speculation has ranged from "Ultra" to "Fold" to the somewhat playful "iFlip," but until Apple makes it official, these are just educated guesses wrapped in optimism.

What's interesting here is the restraint in color options for the foldable. If true, it suggests Apple views foldables as a more premium, conservative product category compared to the mainstream Pro lineup. That's a strategic choice worth noting.

## The Bigger Picture on [Technology](https://infeeds.com/tags/?tag=technology)

Apple's annual color refresh has become almost as predictable as the September launch event. The company dangles a new shade to keep the flagship feeling fresh without requiring major hardware overhauls. Dark cherry, if it's real, would be a smart move for a brand that's already mastered the art of making incremental upgrades feel essential.

The question isn't really whether dark cherry looks good. It probably does. The question is whether rumors like this actually move the needle on consumer interest, or if they've simply become background noise in the endless churn of Apple speculation. A report this detailed, complete with Pantone codes and supplier information, feels like it has teeth. But Apple's legendary secrecy means we won't know for sure until the company decides to tell us.

For now, dark cherry remains a rumor dressed up in plausible details. Whether that's enough to believe it is up to you.
