Mack Keane didn't set out to become one of contemporary R&B's most sought-after collaborators. Yet the LA-bred singer-songwriter has quietly embedded himself in some of the genre's most exciting recent projects, from Destin Conrad's Grammy-nominated Love on Digital to Joyce Wrice's Overgrown. His work as a songwriter, producer, and vocalist has helped shape the sound of an ascendant pocket of modern soul.

But those credits, impressive as they are, were never meant to overshadow Keane's own artistry. After years of self-produced early EPs and collaborative projects, he's finally arrived at what he considers his true debut: Wide Eyed, released May 8 via Rob Walker's relaunched Star Trak Entertainment.

## From Background to Foreground

Keane's journey to Wide Eyed wasn't linear. Born into LA's creative ecosystem, the 29-year-old grew up surrounded by music. His mother is singer-actress Paula Mulcahy, his father is Grammy-nominated songwriter-producer Tom Keane, and his grandfather, Bob Keane, founded Del-Fi Records, which released Ritchie Valens's iconic "La Bamba" single. Music wasn't a career choice; it was the air he breathed.

His early solo work between 2016 and 2019 felt, by his own admission, unfocused. "I was just making songs because I wanted to express myself in that way," he recalls. Everything shifted when he connected with Filipino-American producer ESTA. for 2022's Intersections. Suddenly, Keane understood his own voice.

After working across other artists' projects and finally getting clarity on his vision, Keane initially planned a straightforward solo follow-up. Instead, life happened. A mixtape called Entries arrived in 2025 as a more immediate emotional purge before he settled into making Wide Eyed with collaborators Austin Daniel Brown and his twin John Sterling Brown, who drums throughout the project.

## A Record About Becoming

Wide Eyed isn't just an album; it's a document of Keane stepping into manhood. The record weaves together lessons from his 20s, exploring relationships, vulnerability, and the sometimes painful work of knowing what you actually want. "I would get excited about feelings and emotions but not think too far in the future about what that really means," he explains. "As we get older, shit gets more real."

The album balances Stevie Wonder-esque chords and Fleetwood Mac guitars alongside sparse, funky grooves. On tracks like "Honeymoon Dreaming" and "Bloodshot," Keane channels blue-eyed soul while nodding to hip-hop and funk sensibilities that shaped his LA upbringing. "Bloodshot" especially marks a turning point, made in just six hours and stripped of Auto-Tune to capture raw emotion over technical perfection.

The title itself evolved during the creative process. Initially, Keane planned something darker, but photographer Jack McKain pointed out that the album's sentiment transcended failure. It's hopeful but naive, honest but learning. Wide Eyed captures that duality perfectly. The closer, "Fantasize," was actually one of the last tracks recorded, nodding back to "Bloodshot" and pointing forward to what Keane plans next.

## Already Thinking Ahead

As his Wide Eyed Tour wraps this summer and his guest appearance on Chris Patrick's "Run It Back" gains traction in hip-hop circles, Keane isn't resting. He's already filling voice memos with ideas for album number two. His relationship with Destin Conrad continues to fuel his growth, with Conrad bringing him on tour and creating a genuine creative friendship born from an Insecure soundtrack writing camp back in 2021.

On stage, Keane gushes about audiences singing every lyric, about performing alongside the Browns who helped birth this album. Success, he defines, isn't a destination but an active process: creating with people he loves, maintaining freedom, and eventually headlining Coachella or doing stadium runs. He even entertains the idea of becoming "the Anthony Bourdain of music and travel," blending his love of nature and exploration into his work.

For now, though, Wide Eyed stands as proof that patience and collaboration matter more than early hype or family legacy. Mack Keane has finally stepped fully into the artist he is.
